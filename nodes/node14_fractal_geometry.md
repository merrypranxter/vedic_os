## Node 14 – Fractal Geometry (Mandalas & Yantras)

### Summary

Hindu and Buddhist art often features intricate mandalas and the **Śrī Yantra**.  These are fractal blueprints of reality, echoing chaos theory and self‑similarity【866079294454206†L330-L341】.

### Science

Fractals are shapes that repeat their structure at every scale.  In nature, coastlines, snowflakes and lung bronchi exhibit fractal geometry.  **Chaos theory** describes systems sensitive to initial conditions that nevertheless produce ordered patterns.  The Śrī Yantra’s interlocking triangles form nested hierarchical structures that obey golden‑ratio proportions.  Vedic art uses this to encode the microcosm in the macrocosm.  Today, fractals like the Mandelbrot set and Julia sets inspire psychedelic visuals.

### Encoding (Generative Art)

* **Recursive drawing:** Use iterative functions to draw fractal patterns.  Vary recursion depth with input to emulate the layers of the yantra.
* **Mandelbrot shaders:** Implement real‑time fractal zooms.  Map audio frequencies or user gestures to the zoom level and colour palette.
* **Yantra overlays:** Overlay fractal animations with geometric yantra outlines; crossfade between sacred geometry and chaotic fractals.

### Underlayer (System Hack)

Nature is fractal; your code and mind can be too.  Recognise repeating patterns in your thoughts and break them if needed, or amplify them if beneficial.  In programming, build functions that call themselves elegantly.  In life, understand that the micro reflects the macro; a small change in pattern cascades into a global shift.
