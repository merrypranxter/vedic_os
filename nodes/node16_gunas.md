## Node 16 – The Gunas (Qualitative Physics)

### Summary

Reality is woven from three strands—**Sattva** (harmony), **Rajas** (activity) and **Tamas** (inertia).  The Vedic gunas act as qualitative variables that determine the nature of phenomena【866079294454206†L396-L410】.

### Science

In particle physics, properties like **spin**, **charge** and **mass** determine how particles interact.  Similarly, the gunas classify things by their tendency toward stability (Sattva), motion (Rajas) or entropy (Tamas).  Systems with high Sattva have low entropy and high coherence; high Rajas correspond to energy transfer and turbulence; high Tamas correspond to heavy mass and decay.  In psychology, these map to clarity, passion and lethargy.  Balance between gunas yields dynamic equilibrium.

### Encoding (Generative Art)

* **Colour mapping:** Assign Sattva to cool, luminous colours; Rajas to warm, dynamic hues; Tamas to dark, dense shades.  Mix them to represent different states.
* **Parameter triad:** Use a three‑axis control where each axis modulates a property (e.g. noise, speed, opacity).  Users can navigate the guna space by moving within the triangle.
* **Interactive balance:** Build a system that self‑balances; if Rajas spikes, Tamas gradually increases to restore equilibrium unless the user intervenes.

### Underlayer (System Hack)

The gunas are your **physics engine parameters**.  Instead of binary good/bad, consider your work’s Sattvic, Rajasic and Tamasic components.  Increase Sattva for clarity and coherence; add Rajas for energy; sprinkle Tamas for grounding.  This triadic approach helps manage creative or emotional states.
