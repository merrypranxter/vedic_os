### Science

The age of the Earth is about 4.54 billion years, close to the Vedic **kalpa** (4.32 billion years).  Brahmā lives for 100 years of such days and nights, totalling roughly 311 trillion years, comparable to current predictions of the “Big Freeze” timescale or proton decay.  The cycle of creation and dissolution in Hindu cosmology maps to cosmic expansion, heat death and potential big crunch or bounce.  The concept of enormous durations encourages thinking beyond human lifespans and fosters a cosmic perspective.

### Underlayer (System Hack)

You operate on human timescales but exist in a cosmic context.  Recognising the vastness of time reduces the weight of trivial events.  In code, think about run‑time efficiency for long processes; in life, design projects that may outlive you.  Use cosmic time as a meditation: what does your creative work mean on a billion‑year timescale?
