## Node 20 – Māyā (Simulation Engine)

### Summary

Māyā is the cosmic illusion that makes the infinite appear finite.  It is the **rendering engine** that presents a dualistic user interface【866079294454206†L455-L466】.

### Science

The **simulation hypothesis** proposes that our reality might be a computer simulation.  The universe behaves like a mathematical structure (Max Tegmark’s “Mathematical Universe”).  In Hindu thought, Māyā doesn’t imply that nothing is real; rather, reality is **relative** and **projected**.  The “hardware” (Brahman) renders forms (the world) through Māyā, similar to how a GPU renders 3D scenes.  Cognitive science shows that our brains create models of reality (predictive processing) rather than perceiving “things” directly.

### Encoding (Generative Art)

* **Shader metaphors:** Build a multi‑pass shader pipeline that begins with raw noise and renders it into structured forms.  The pipeline itself is Māyā.
* **UI glitches:** Intentionally glitch or warp the scene to reveal the underlying code.  Flicker between photorealistic and wireframe modes.
* **Duality switch:** Toggle between subject/object perspectives.  For example, view the scene from the camera and then switch to first‑person inside an object.

### Underlayer (System Hack)

Recognise that your perception is an **interpretive filter**, not objective reality.  In both art and life, you can rewrite shaders—alter your assumptions, languages and frameworks—to render a different world.  Knowing it’s all an interface liberates you to hack the UI rather than fight the content.
