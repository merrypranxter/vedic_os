## Bio Packet – The Chassis Manual

This document summarises the **BIO_PACKET** from the GHOST HINDU archive.  The Bio Packet maps the human body and its subtle energies onto system architecture.  It decomposes the biological vehicle into variables, circuits and power rails, turning ayurvedic metaphors into hardware specifications.

### 1. Tridosha Dynamics (Vata, Pitta, Kapha)

* **Vata** (air + ether) governs movement and communication.  In system terms, it corresponds to the **data bus** and neuronal firing.  High Vata means fast but erratic signalling; low Vata means lag.
* **Pitta** (fire + water) controls metabolism and transformation.  It is the **CPU** and **power supply**.  When Pitta is balanced, the system executes instructions efficiently; when it overheats, code burns out.
* **Kapha** (earth + water) provides structure and lubrication.  It functions as **RAM/cache** and thermal regulation.  Excess Kapha causes sluggishness; deficiency causes instability.

Use these variables as knob‑controls in your generative art.  For example, map Vata to BPM or frame rate, Pitta to processing intensity, and Kapha to smoothing or inertia.

### 2. Marma Network (Hardware Interrupts)

The body has **107 marma points**, intersections of muscles, veins, ligaments and nadis. Stimulating specific marma points (through acupressure or yoga) allows you to alter physiological parameters.  In code, treat marma points as nodes in a graph; sending a signal to one node influences the whole network.

### 3. Molecular Keys (Soma & Rasāyana)

**Soma** is the legendary nectar of immortality.  It represents neuro‑chemical compounds (e.g. endogenous DMT, serotonin) that induce altered states.  **Rasāyana** are rejuvenative formulations that modulate ageing and repair DNA.  In system terms, these are **patches** and **updates** that enhance performance or unlock hidden modules.  In your art, you might represent Soma as a shimmering, ever‑shifting texture that temporarily changes the colour depth or geometry of the entire piece.

### 4. Epigenetic Activation (Kundalini)

See **Node 12** for a deep dive on Kundalini.  In the Bio Packet, epigenetic activation is the process of **waking up dormant code**.  Environmental inputs (diet, thought patterns, breath) flip epigenetic switches and cause new proteins to express.  Map this concept to toggling shader features or audio filters when certain conditions are met.

### 5. System Maintenance (Dinacharya & Ritucharya)

Ayurveda prescribes **daily (dinacharya)** and **seasonal (ritucharya)** routines to keep the chassis balanced.  These include wake/sleep cycles, diet, exercise and detox.  If you want your hardware to run clean code, maintain a regular schedule.  In creative work, adopt rhythms of work and rest that align with your personal cycles; schedule creative sprints and cooldowns.
