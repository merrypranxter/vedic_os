## Node 9 – Multiverse (Brane Cosmology)

### Summary

The Vedic notion of countless universes floating like bubbles in cosmic waters anticipates modern **multiverse** theories.  Each Brahmā sits on a lotus emerging from Viṣṇu’s navel; there are infinite lotuses.

### Science

**M‑theory** and **brane cosmology** suggest that our universe is a four‑dimensional membrane embedded in a higher‑dimensional space.  Collisions between branes might cause big bangs.  Inflationary cosmology also allows for pocket universes with different physical constants.  Vedic texts speak of infinite Brahmāṇḍas (cosmic eggs) and 14 lokas stacked in a cosmic ocean.  These analogies illustrate that reality may be part of a bigger multiversal foam.

### Encoding (Generative Art)

* **Bubble foam:** Render a 3D field of translucent spheres floating in a fluid.  Each bubble contains a tiny universe with unique shaders and physics.
* **Brane collision:** Animate two planar surfaces colliding, spawning a burst of particles (big bang).  Change the parameters of the universe after each collision.
* **Lotus arrays:** Use patterns of lotus flowers or fractal kaleidoscopes with multiple centres to represent parallel cosmoses.

### Underlayer (System Hack)

The simulation may be running multiple instances.  Recognise that parameters like physical constants or algorithm seeds can vary across universes.  In creative work, spawn multiple versioned worlds.  In consciousness, entertain the possibility that your reality is one of many, making you less attached to any single configuration.
