## Node 15 – Higher Dimensional Planes (14 Lokas)

### Summary

The Vedas speak of **fourteen lokas**—planes of existence stacked from hellish realms to heavenly spheres.  This anticipates theories of extra dimensions and parallel branes【866079294454206†L343-L357】.

### Science

**Kaluza–Klein theory** and **superstring theory** extend spacetime beyond our familiar four dimensions.  M‑theory requires 11 dimensions for mathematical consistency.  Extra dimensions may be compactified, hidden from everyday experience.  The lokas—ranging from Bhūloka (Earth) to Satyaloka (highest heaven)—can be mapped to different vibration bands or “frequencies” of reality.  Each loka is effectively its own **brane** with its own physics.  The idea that consciousness can ascend through these planes mirrors the notion that objects can traverse or resonate with higher dimensions.

### Encoding (Generative Art)

* **Layered scenes:** Stack multiple visual layers representing different lokas.  Users can shift between them via input, with each layer having distinct colour palettes and physics rules.
* **Dimensional extrusion:** Animate objects “popping” into and out of higher dimensions, leaving behind shadows or projections.
* **Frequency mapping:** Assign each loka a specific frequency band (audio or colour).  Moving the dial shifts the entire scene into a different band.

Reality has layers you seldom perceive.  With the right tuning—altering your vibration (meditation, breathwork, software frequency)—you can access higher or lower states.  In code, use multi‑layered architectures (e.g. parallax, multi‑scene) to simulate hidden dimensions; in life, be aware of context switching between social, mental and spiritual “planes.”
