## Node 4 – Particle Physics (Vaisheshika Sutra)

### Summary

Long before modern physics, Indian sages proposed an atomic theory.  The **Vaisheshika Sutra** describes particles (paramāṇu), their combinations and the properties of matter【866079294454206†L90-L98】.

### Science

The Vaisheshika school (c. 6th century BCE) enumerates seven categories: substance (dravyā), quality (guṇa), motion (karma), universal (sāmānya), particular (viśeṣa), inherence (samavāya) and non‑existence (abhāva).  Atoms combine to form molecules with emergent properties.  This parallels modern particle physics, which identifies quarks, leptons and gauge bosons, and uses **quantum chromodynamics** to explain how they bind into matter.  The ancients even classified sound, taste and colour as inherent qualities of atoms.

* **Paramāṇu poetry:** Convert the sutras into generative audio or text that spawns particle systems in response to syllables.

Each dravyā is a class, each guṇa is an attribute, and karma is a method.  By aligning your creative code with these categories, you create “procedurally spiritual” art.
