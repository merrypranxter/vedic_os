## Node 11 – Time Dilation (Kakudmi’s Paradox)

### Summary

King **Kakudmi** visited Brahmā for a brief meeting and returned to find that ages had passed on Earth.  This story illustrates **relativistic time dilation**【866079294454206†L270-L297】.

### Science

In **general relativity**, time runs slower in stronger gravitational fields or at high velocities.  Astronauts age slightly less than people on Earth due to both gravitational and kinematic effects.  The Kakudmi tale models this: time on Brahmā’s plane (a higher loka) is dilated compared to Earth.  The ratio of time experienced is roughly 4.32 billion human years per day of Brahmā, aligning with cosmological cycles.  This is a mythic articulation of the idea that **time is relative to frame of reference**.

### Encoding (Generative Art)

* **Nested clocks:** Visualise multiple clocks ticking at different speeds.  When the viewer interacts, zoom between them to show relative time flows.
* **Gravitational lens:** Use a shader that slows down animations near massive objects.  Drop in heavy spheres to warp the animation timeline.
* **Relativity warp:** Map audio or input speed to a parameter that stretches/compresses time variables in the visuals.

### Underlayer (System Hack)

Time is not absolute; it’s a variable you can modulate.  In the myth, visiting higher dimensions changes your update rate.  In daily life, change your environment (altitude, mental state, data bandwidth) to alter your subjective time.  In code, treat time as a parameter accessible via modulation, not a fixed constant.
