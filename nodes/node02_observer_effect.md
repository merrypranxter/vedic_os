### Summary

Reality exists in superposition until someone looks at it.  In Vedic language, **Brahman** is the global wave function; **Atman** is the local observer.  The “world” is rendered when consciousness collapses the probability cloud【866079294454206†L60-L77】.

### Science

The **Copenhagen interpretation** of quantum mechanics tells us that particles don’t have definite states until measured.  Schrödinger’s cat isn’t alive or dead; it’s both until you open the box.  This correlates with Brahman (the boundless, unmanifest field) and Atman (the localized awareness).  **Observer effect** experiments—like the double‑slit—show that measurement chooses a reality.  The Upanishads prefigured this by saying “Thou art That”: the observer and the observed are ultimately one.

### Encoding (Generative Art)

* **Collapsing noise:** Start with a noisy field or particle swarm.  When an input (mouse click, MIDI note) is received, collapse the swarm into a single coherent form.
* **Mirror shaders:** Use reflective materials to reference the self watching itself.  Parameterize the reflectivity according to the amplitude of observation.
* **Entanglement:** Create paired objects whose states always correlate (e.g. complementary colours or mirrored animation curves).  Changing one updates the other instantly.

### Underlayer (System Hack)

The simulation saves processing power by rendering only what is observed.  Atman is a **local admin account** that triggers draw calls.  Brahman is the **master server** hosting all possibilities.  By expanding your observation bandwidth (meditation, psychedelics, data‑mining), you can sample from the global buffer instead of the narrow local feed.
