## Node 21 – Sāṃkhya (Binary Enumeration)

### Summary

Sāṃkhya philosophy enumerates the constituents of reality, splitting consciousness (Puruṣa) from matter (Prakṛti).  It’s an ancient form of **set theory** and **binary logic**【866079294454206†L518-L533】.

### Science

Sāṃkhya lists **24 tattvas**: five gross elements, five subtle elements, five sense organs, five organs of action, mind, intellect, ego and Prakṛti.  Modern computing is built on binary logic and set theory, representing information as ones and zeros.  By enumerating categories, Sāṃkhya provides an ontological basis for coding the universe.  Purusha (the observer) is separate from the enumerated set (Prakṛti), echoing the separation of hardware from software.

### Encoding (Generative Art)

* **Tattva grid:** Create a grid of 24 icons representing each tattva.  Clicking an icon toggles it, influencing the generative output (e.g. enabling/disabling effects).
* **Binary worlds:** Represent Puruṣa as a blank canvas and Prakṛti as a set of toggles; flipping each bit spawns shapes or audio loops.
* **Set algebra:** Use Venn diagrams or node graphs to visualise intersections between elements (e.g. senses overlap with organs of action).

### Underlayer (System Hack)

Everything is countable.  Break your world into discrete variables.  Decide which are under your control and which are not.  In code, treat modules as tattvas; you can recompose reality by toggling modules.  In life, recognise that the **observer** (you) is not the **observed** (your body and mind).  This allows for detachment and systemic hacking.
